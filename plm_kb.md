# PLM Approval Process for Engineering Changes

A Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) system uses a structured workflow to evaluate, validate, 
and implement product modifications. 
This process protects product quality and prevents manufacturing disruptions.

### 1. Change Request Creation (ECR)
* **Identify:** A user finds a defect, cost-saving step, or compliance issue.
* **Document:** The user creates an Engineering Change Request (ECR) in PLM.
* **Attach:** The user links affected items like CAD files and drawings.

### 2. Initial Review and Triage
* **Assign:** A change analyst or product manager reviews the incoming ECR.
* **Filter:** The reviewer rejects duplicate, incomplete, or unfeasible requests.
* **Prioritize:** The request gets a priority level based on urgency.

### 3. Impact Analysis
* **Assess:** Engineering, manufacturing, sourcing, and quality teams review the ECR.
* **Calculate:** Teams estimate costs for scrap, tooling, and new parts.
* **Trace:** PLM identifies every parent assembly or product line affected.

### 4. Change Order Formalization (ECO)
* **Promote:** The authorized ECR becomes an Engineering Change Order (ECO).
* **Plan:** The ECO defines the exact solution and effective date.
* **Task:** PLM distributes design and rework tasks to engineers.

### 5. Design and Validation
* **Modify:** Engineers update the CAD models and Bills of Materials (BOM).
* **Verify:** Teams run simulations or build prototypes to test changes.
* **Status:** PLM data status moves from "In Work" to "Under Review."

### 6. Change Control Board (CCB) Approval
* **Convene:** A cross-functional committee (CCB) reviews the complete ECO package.
* **Vote:** Members approve, reject, or request changes inside PLM.
* **Sign-off:** The system captures electronic signatures for official regulatory compliance.

### 7. Implementation and Release
* **Publish:** PLM releases the new revision and obsoletes old data.
* **Sync:** PLM pushes the updated BOM data to the ERP system.
* **Execute:** Factory floors update instructions and sourcing contacts suppliers.
